New York

Yankee Stadium

Yankee Stadium is a stadium located in Bronx in New York City. It serves as the home ballpark for the New York Yankees. Did you know: After the 2016 season, the Yankees began doing updates to Yankee Stadium including the Masterpass Batter's Eye Deck and Frank's Red Hot Terrace and Toyota Terrace..

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a colossal neoclassical sculpture on Liberty Island in New York Harbor in New York City, in the United States. Did you know: After closing the interior of the Statue of Liberty due to the attacks of September 11, 2001, the National Parks Service resumed public access starting on July 4, 2009.

Time Square

The Theater District is the teeming heart of Midtown West. In the pedestrian plazas of Times Square, costumed characters beckon to energetic crowds under the pulsing lights of towering digital billboards.

Cleveland

Progressive Field

Progressive Field is a baseball park located in the downtown area of Cleveland, Ohio, United States. Home to the Cleveland Indians Did you know: Progressive Field is one of the few facilities in baseball history to host the Major League Baseball All-Star Game and games of the World Series in the same season, which occurred in 1997.

Rock & Roll HOF

Located on the shore of Lake Erie in downtown Cleveland, Ohio. The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Foundation was established on April 20, 1983, by Atlantic Records founder and chairman Ahmet Ertegun. Did you know: The Paul Butterfield Blues Band was first nominated for the Rock Hall in 2006, but has been eligible since 1988.

Westside Market

The West Side Market is the oldest operating indoor/outdoor market space in Cleveland, Ohio. It is located at the corner of West 25th Street and Lorain Avenue in the Ohio City neighborhood.

Chicago

Wrigley Field

Wrigley Field is a baseball park located on the North Side of Chicago, Illinois. It is the home of the Chicago Cubs, one of the city's two Major League Baseball franchises. Did you know: The CTA red line takes you directly to Wrigley Field, get off at Addison.

The Navy Pier

Navy Pier is a 3,300-foot-long pier on the Chicago shoreline of Lake Michigan. It is located in the Streeterville neighborhood of the Near North Side community area Did you know: Navy Pier opened to the public on July 15, 1916.

The Bean Sculpture

The Cloud Gate Sculpture, also known as the "bean," is one of the highlights of Millennium Park of Chicago, Illinois. Designed by Anish Kapoor, the Chicago the Bean sculpture is made of 168 highly polished stainless steel plates - giving the appearance of liquid mercury. Did you know: Visitors are able to walk around and under Cloud Gate's 12-foot (3.7 m) high arch.
